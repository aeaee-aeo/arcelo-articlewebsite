Seventeen named UNESCO's first Goodwill Ambassador for Youth

K-pop group Seventeen has made history as the first K-pop artist to be named UNESCO's Goodwill Ambassador for Youth. The group officially received this honor during a ceremony at UNESCO's headquarters in Paris, where they delivered a heartfelt acceptance speech.

Joshua, a Los Angeles-born member, spoke on behalf of the group, expressing their gratitude and emphasizing their commitment to empowering young people. He noted that their music reflects their own experiences and emotions, which has helped them connect with youth globally.

Source: TheKoreaTimes

As part of their role, Seventeen announced they would donate $1 million to the "Global Youth Grant Scheme," a joint initiative with UNESCO to support educational opportunities for young people worldwide. This effort is a continuation of their #GoingTogether campaign, launched in 2022, which aims to highlight the importance of education and foster global participation in educational activities.

Seventeen’s agency, Pledis Entertainment, highlighted the group’s global influence and their consistent message of resilience and growth, communicated through their music. The group has previously supported educational initiatives, such as establishing two community learning centers in East Timor in collaboration with the Korean National Commission for UNESCO.

Since their debut in 2015 with the EP 17 Carat, Seventeen has achieved global success, with hit songs like "Very Nice" and "Don't Wanna Cry." Their latest album, 17 IS RIGHT HERE, released in April, sold over 2.9 million copies in its first week and debuted at No. 5 on the Billboard 200 chart.

Through their new role, Seventeen aims to inspire youth worldwide, emphasizing the importance of companionship and collective action to build supportive global communities.
